# Research in entomology

My research interests are focused on the **diversity of Neotropical insects**, especially the order Coleoptera. During my carreer, I have had the opoortunity of working with different groups, but currently I am focused on the taxonomy and systematics of the family **Anthribidae** (Coleoptera: Curculionoidea). My research is develped mainly in entomological collections, but I have also participated in a broad number of collecting trips, especially in Guatemala. One of my first fieldrtrips was as a student participant in the Leaf Litter Arthropods of Mesoamerica (LLAMA) project, leaded by Dr. Jack Longino, then from Evergreen State College, and Dr. Robert Anderson, from the Canadian Museum of Nature.

## Guatemala

The study of the biodiversity of Guatemala is one of my main interests. I have collaborated actively with the main [entomological collections](http://insectosdeguatemala.org/colecciones/) in the country since I was an undergrad. Many of the specimes I have collected during several reserach projects, are deposited in the collections. One of the most relevant projects during this time was my B.S. [dissertation](http://www.repositorio.usac.edu.gt/2036/1/06_3652.pdf), that was focused on the diversity of fungivorous Coleoptera of the National Park Laguna Lachuá, in Northern Guatemala. As a part of this research, I had the oportunity to work with the preparation and identification of several families collected on fungi, mailny Staphylinidae, Ptiliidae, Erotylidae y Nitidulidae. A [poster](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/322702818_Coleopteros_asociados_a_Marasmiaceae_y_Polyporaceae_en_la_selva_lluviosa_de_Lachua_Guatemala) was presented with the findings, during the VIII Latin American Mycology Meeting in Colombia. My dissertation was awarded as the best of the Biology program in San Carlos University, in 2014, and was [published](https://zenodo.org/record/3968462#.YcyzklllCUl) the next year along my advisor at the time, Dr. Maura Quezada, specialist in macrofungi.

After finishing my degree in Biology, I continued doing entomological research in the private sector, and kept collaborating with the entomological collections by depositing the vouchers of different projects. In 2015, I edited, along Dr. Jiichiro Yoshimoto and Dr. Enio Cano (†), the book "Insects of Guatemala" (in Spanish), which has turned in a relevant tool for the entomology students in the country. In 2018 the second edition was published with some updates, and in 2020, I published the website ["Insects of Guatemala"](http://insectosdeguatemala.org/), to share additional information about the insect groups and the entomological collections in the country.

## Panama

From 2016 to 2018, I was awarded with a [DAAD](https://www.daad-costa-rica.org/es/encontrar-becas/becas-en-costa-rica-y-region/programa-regional/) scholarship and I had the opportunity to study a M.S. in Entomology in Panama. During my time in that country, I continued with the research on fungivorous Coleoptera, and I published a small [paper](https://zenodo.org/record/3968793#.Ycy1JlllCUk) with data from the Metropolitan Natural Park, in Panama City. There is no need to say that the diversity of Coleoptera and Fungi was impressive in this locality. With only six hours of sampling, I was able to collect more than 3,500 specimens in twelve families, including Staphylinidae, Ciidae, Erotylidae y Nitidulidae.

Later, with the motivation of my then advisor, Dr. Héctor Barrios, I focused on a group that was not includen in the fungivorous Coleoptera I collected, but where abundand (and undetermined) in the collections: the family Anthribidae, the "fungus weevils". My dissertation consisted in the revision of the species of Anthribidae reported in Panama to generate an updated catalogue and a key for the genera of the country. This work was published later.

## United States

I arrived to Arizona in 2019, where I am currently pursuing a PhD on Evolutionary Biology, in the lab of [Dr. Nico Franz](https://sols.asu.edu/nico-franz). From 2019 to 2021 I worked as a Research Assistant in the Biodiversity Knowledge Integration Center, processing and identifying samples of the project "Weevils of Sonora". I also continued with the construction of the catalogue of the Anthribidae of Panama, now that I had access to the incredible O'Brien collection, with more than two million specimens, including around 20,000 anthribids. Later, I was able to visit the collections at the Smithsonian National Museum of Natural History, and I was able to complete the catalogue for its publication. (Go to the [Anthribidae](https://ksorellana.github.io/es/anthribidae) section to see more).

At ASU I also learned how to digitize entomological specimens in [ecdysis](https://serv.biokic.asu.edu/ecdysis/), one of the [Symbiota](symbiota.org) portals developed and mantained by this institution. Besides digitize the specimens of the ASU Collections ([ASUCOB](https://serv.biokic.asu.edu/ecdysis/collections/misc/collprofiles.php?collid=2), [ASUHIC](https://serv.biokic.asu.edu/ecdysis/collections/misc/collprofiles.php?collid=1)), I was able to obtain a profile for my own research collection [(SOAC)](https://serv.biokic.asu.edu/ecdysis/collections/misc/collprofiles.php?collid=20), also on [GBIF](https://www.gbif.org/dataset/d1302b60-c820-4f68-a1bd-8bb0c167c44c). In, 2020 I proposed the digitization of entomological collections in Guatemala, which led to the creation of the [Guatemala Biodiversity Symbiota Portal](https://biodiversidad.gt/)  (Go to the [Digitization](https://ksorellana.github.io/es/digitalizacion) section to see more).
